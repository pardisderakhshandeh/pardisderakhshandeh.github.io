Hi, welcome to my website.

I am a PhD candidate in the [Department of Linguistics at Stony Brook University](https://www.stonybrook.edu/commcms/linguistics/), advised by Dr. Paco Ordóñez. 

My research focuses on formal syntax and morphology, specifically complex alignment and agreement systems. I primarily work on **Behbahani**, an endangered Southwestern Iranian language. My dissertation investigates how argument structure, verbal stems, agreement, and alignment interact in Behbahani grammar, paying special attention to split alignment in a language that lacks morphological case.

My work integrates three connected areas:
* **Formal Syntactic Analysis:** Using Behbahani to ask broader theoretical questions about argument licensing, alignment, and the syntax-morphology interface.
* **Language Documentation:** Making an endangered, primarily oral language more visible within and beyond linguistics.
* **Computational Modeling:** Developing Haskell-based finite-state automata (FSA) models to formally capture Behbahani agreement patterns.

You can find more about my research, teaching, and CV through the links on this website.
