    <p>
      I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Linguistics at Stony Brook University.
      My research is in syntax and morphology, with a focus on Iranian languages and especially
      Behbahani, a Southwestern Iranian language spoken in southwestern Iran.
    </p>

    <p>
      My dissertation investigates how verbal morphology relates to syntactic structure. I am interested in
      how patterns involving stems, argument indexing, and non-finite forms can help us understand the
      structure of the verbal domain.
    </p>

    <p>
      Alongside formal analysis, I am involved in language documentation and resource-building for
      underdocumented languages. My work combines theoretical linguistics with fieldwork, corpus development,
      and computational modeling.
    </p>
