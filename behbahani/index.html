Behbahani

About Behbahani

A Southwestern Iranian language from Behbahan, Iran

Behbahani is a Southwestern Iranian language spoken primarily in Behbahan, in southwestern Iran. It is a primarily oral language with no standardized writing system and limited publicly available linguistic resources. This page provides a brief overview of the language, its grammatical profile, and ongoing work to document and analyze it.

Language profile

Behbahani belongs to the Iranian branch of the Indo-Iranian languages. It is used as a local and community language in and around Behbahan, while Persian is the dominant language of education, administration, media, and public writing.

Family

Indo-European, Indo-Iranian, Iranian, Southwestern Iranian.

Location

Primarily associated with Behbahan, in southwestern Iran.

Writing

Primarily oral, with no widely standardized orthography.

Underdocumented, with limited descriptive and digital resources.

Grammatical features at a glance

Behbahani is especially interesting for formal linguistics because agreement and alignment do not follow from any single principle: they shift depending on tense, transitivity, and argument structure, and interact with stem alternations in ways that are not visible from surface forms alone. My work focuses on how these patterns can be analyzed at the syntax-morphology interface.

Pro-drop Subjects can often be omitted when their reference is recoverable from verbal morphology and context.
No morphological case The language does not mark arguments with overt morphological case, which makes its indexing patterns theoretically important.
Split alignment Argument indexing differs across past and non-past environments, creating a split alignment pattern.
Stem alternations Past and non-past stems interact with tense, aspect, transitivity, and non-finite morphology.
Agreement patterns Subject and object indexing are sensitive to tense, transitivity, and whether certain arguments are overtly expressed.
Nominalization Agentive, resultative, and infinitival forms provide evidence about the size and structure of verbal domains.

Why Behbahani matters

Behbahani matters for two connected reasons. Empirically, it is an underdocumented Iranian language whose grammar has not been fully represented in the descriptive or digital record. Theoretically, it offers a case where the mechanisms behind complex alignment can be studied directly, without the interference of overt case morphology that complicates this question in many other languages.

As a native speaker and linguistic researcher, I approach Behbahani both as a language that deserves documentation and as a grammatical system that can contribute to broader debates in syntax and morphology.
