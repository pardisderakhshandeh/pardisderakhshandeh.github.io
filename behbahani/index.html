Behbahani

About Behbahani

An underdocumented Southwestern Iranian language from Behbahan, Iran

Behbahani is a Southwestern Iranian language spoken primarily in and around Behbahan, in southwestern Iran. Although it is historically related to Persian, it has its own grammatical profile, including distinctive patterns of agreement, argument indexing, stem alternations, and nominalization. This page introduces the language, its linguistic profile, and ongoing work to document and analyze it.

Why Behbahani matters

Behbahani matters both empirically and theoretically. Empirically, it is an underdocumented Iranian language whose grammar has not been fully represented in the descriptive or digital record. Theoretically, it offers important evidence for how complex agreement and alignment systems are built, especially in a language without overt morphological case.

Iranian linguistics

Behbahani contributes to the study of Southwestern Iranian languages and helps document grammatical diversity within the Iranian language family.

Agreement and alignment

Its agreement patterns make it possible to study split alignment without relying on overt case morphology.

Morphology and stems

The interaction of past and non-past stems with verbal morphology offers evidence for the structure of verbal domains.

Documentation and technology

Work on Behbahani can support language documentation, digital resources, and computational modeling for underrepresented languages.

As a native speaker and linguistic researcher, I approach Behbahani both as a language that deserves documentation and as a grammatical system that can contribute to broader debates in syntax and morphology.

Language profile

Behbahani is used as a local and community language in and around Behbahan, while Persian functions as the dominant language of formal education, administration, media, and most written communication. Like many underdocumented languages, Behbahani is primarily transmitted through speech and everyday community use rather than through standardized schooling or public writing.

Community use

Behbahani is primarily used in local, family, and community contexts. More detailed sociolinguistic information will be added as documentation develops.

Orality

The language is primarily oral and does not currently have a widely standardized spelling system.

Contact with Persian

Behbahani exists alongside Persian, which shapes literacy, schooling, media exposure, and public language use.

Documentation

Current work includes elicitation, grammatical description, naturalistic speech data, and syntactic analysis.

Grammatical sketch

Behbahani has several grammatical properties that make it valuable for descriptive, theoretical, and computational work. The points below provide a brief overview. Fuller descriptions and examples will be added as this page develops.

Pro-drop Subjects can often be omitted when their reference is recoverable from verbal morphology and context.
No overt case marking Arguments are not marked by overt morphological case, which makes the language especially useful for studying indexing and alignment.
Split alignment Argument indexing differs across past and non-past environments, producing a split alignment pattern.
Agreement patterns Subject and object indexing are sensitive to tense, transitivity, and whether arguments are overtly expressed.
Stem alternations Past and non-past stems interact with tense, aspect, transitivity, causation, and non-finite morphology.
Nominalization Agentive, resultative, and infinitival forms provide evidence about the size and structure of verbal domains.
