Behbahani

Southwestern Iranian · Syntax · Morphology

Behbahani as a window into alignment, stems, and argument structure

Behbahani is a Southwestern Iranian language spoken primarily in Behbahan, Iran. It is a primarily oral language with no standardized writing system and limited descriptive or digital resources. My work on Behbahani combines formal syntactic and morphological analysis with documentation-oriented goals, aiming to make the language more visible in theoretical linguistics and in computationally usable formats.

Why Behbahani matters

Behbahani is especially important for the study of the syntax-morphology interface because it combines complex verbal indexing, split alignment, stem alternations, and the absence of morphological case. This combination creates a useful testing ground for theories of agreement, Voice, argument structure, and the relation between verbal stems and syntactic structure.

Alignment without case

Behbahani shows alignment-sensitive indexing patterns while lacking morphological case, making it useful for separating agreement from overt case morphology.

Stem-sensitive grammar

Past and non-past stems are not just morphological alternants. They interact with transitivity, aspect, non-finite morphology, and argument indexing.

Underdocumented data

Because the language has limited published description, careful documentation can contribute both to formal theory and to the visibility of minoritized Iranian languages.

Research & core puzzles

My dissertation investigates how argument structure, verbal stems, agreement, and alignment interact in Behbahani grammar. A central question is whether patterns that appear morphological on the surface are better explained through syntactic structure, especially the distribution of Voice and related functional projections.

Split alignment How are subjects and objects indexed across past and non-past environments?
Stem alternations How do past and non-past stems interact with aspect, transitivity, and non-finite morphology?
Voice Which syntactic structures license external arguments in different verbal domains?
Nominalization What do agentive, resultative, and infinitival forms reveal about the size of verbal structure?
Case and agreement How does the language maintain complex indexing patterns without morphological case?

As a native speaker and linguistic researcher, I approach Behbahani both as an object of formal analysis and as an underdocumented language whose documentation matters beyond theoretical linguistics.

Documentation & computational resources

Because Behbahani has limited publicly available documentation, part of this project is dedicated to creating structured resources that can support future descriptive, theoretical, and computational work.

Computational modeling

I am developing Haskell-based finite-state models to represent selected aspects of Behbahani verbal morphology and agreement. These models are intended to make parts of the analysis explicit, testable, and reusable.

Related areas

Iranian linguistics

Behbahani contributes to broader comparative questions about Southwestern Iranian morphosyntax and understudied Iranian varieties.

Syntax-morphology interface

The project asks how much apparent morphology can be derived from independently motivated syntactic structure.
